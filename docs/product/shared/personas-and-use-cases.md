# Personas and Use Cases

## Primary Personas

### Clarity Seeker

A solo user who feels financially responsible but avoids traditional budgeting because it creates homework and guilt. They want to know what is safe to spend today and whether the week still works.

### Shared Household Operator

A user managing spending with a partner, roommate, or family member. They need split handling, reimbursement correctness, and privacy controls so shared finances do not erase private autonomy.

### FIRE-Minded Planner

A user who wants daily or weekly spending decisions connected to longer-term financial independence outcomes without living inside spreadsheets all day.

### Independent Earner

A freelancer or entrepreneur who needs separation between personal and business spending, cash-flow awareness, and later tax projection support.

## Core Use Cases

- decide what is safe to spend today without recategorizing old transactions
- simulate a purchase before making it
- understand whether an event, trip, or irregular expense changes the week or month
- avoid reimbursement distortion after paying for a group expense
- preserve privacy within a shared household context
- see whether upcoming bills and paydays create a cash squeeze
- recover quickly after an overspend using context and guidance instead of shame
