# Personas and Use Cases

## Primary Personas

### Socially Native Clarity Seeker

A solo user who feels financially responsible but avoids traditional budgeting because it creates homework and guilt. They want to know what is safe to spend today, whether the week still works, and how their real-life spending patterns translate into useful stories, not just reports.

### Trip and Event Organizer

A user who plans dinners, trips, birthdays, weekends, festivals, or shared outings. They need a clear event budget, easy split handling, a record of where money was spent, and a polished summary they can revisit or share.

### Shared-Spend Operator

A user managing spending with a partner, roommate, or group. They need split handling, reimbursement correctness, collaborator visibility, and privacy controls so shared money does not erase private autonomy.

### Private Explorer

A user who loves restaurant, city, or travel discovery and wants a spending map and place memory, but is highly sensitive to oversharing financial or location detail.

## Secondary Personas

### FIRE-Lite Planner

A user who wants daily or weekly decisions connected to longer-term outcomes later, but does not want MVP to become a long-horizon planning product.

### Independent Earner

A freelancer or entrepreneur who needs separation between personal and business spending, cash-flow awareness, and later tax projection support.

## Core Use Cases

- decide what is safe to spend today without recategorizing old transactions
- review recent transactions quickly and correct what matters
- understand whether a trip, dinner, or irregular event changes the week or month
- generate a curated digital receipt or summary from an event
- share favorite spots from an event with map links and spending context
- avoid reimbursement distortion after paying for a group expense
- preserve privacy within a shared household or shared event context
- see whether upcoming bills and paydays create a cash squeeze
- spot where spending clusters geographically and category-wise
- receive a private weekly money story with quick actions to stay on track
- recover quickly after an overspend using context and guidance instead of shame
