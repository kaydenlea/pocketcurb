# Glossary

- `Gama`: the updated product brand for the repository's money product direction.
- `Decision layer`: the product logic that converts raw finance activity into actionable guidance.
- `Safe-to-Spend`: the trusted amount a user can spend without breaking near-term obligations, budget posture, or selected goals.
- `Daily Spending Meter`: the daily guidance surface that translates the user's current budget posture into an understandable daily spend signal.
- `Crisis Cushion`: a report or view that shows how much resilience remains before bills, cash-flow stress, or emergency conditions become dangerous.
- `Event or narrative intelligence`: automatic grouping and explanation of spending around trips, life events, or meaningful patterns rather than only categories.
- `Digital receipt`: a curated summary generated from an event or trip that combines budget, top categories, collaborators, and place context into a reusable artifact.
- `Money story`: a private-by-default weekly or period-based recap that highlights spending patterns, drift, wins, and recommended next actions.
- `Spending map`: a map surface that plots transaction-related places for private exploration or curated sharing.
- `Place intelligence`: merchant and location context derived from transactions that helps users revisit, recommend, or understand the places behind spending.
- `Admin work elimination`: the product principle that users should spend less time categorizing, reconciling, and cleaning up.
- `Forward-looking cash flow`: a view of expected inflows, obligations, and spend capacity across the coming days, weeks, and months.
- `Shared household with private autonomy`: a shared-finance model where household coordination exists without eliminating private spaces or private spending contexts.
- `Personal pots`: user-controlled private or semi-private buckets that preserve autonomy inside a shared environment.
- `Artifact-first social`: a social model based on explicit, curated shares rather than an always-on public feed of raw activity.
- `Rollover budgeting`: carrying over under- or over-spend effects into future decision guidance.
