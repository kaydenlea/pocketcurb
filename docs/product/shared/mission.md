# Mission

Gama exists to turn personal finance from homework into decision support and to turn real spending into useful memory, place context, and optional shareable artifacts. The product should minimize categorization work, handle shared spending correctly, and shift the experience from retrospective cleanup toward forward-looking decisions and socially useful outcomes.

## Canonical Mission Statement

Give people short-term money clarity without shame or admin work, then help them turn real-life spending into trusted guidance, event memory, and shareable artifacts they actually want to keep.

## Mission Candidates to Preserve

1. Build the product that minimizes categorization work, handles reimbursements and shared spending correctly, and shifts the experience from retrospective cleanup to forward-looking decisions.
2. Add a privacy-bounded social layer that helps users relive, recommend, and understand trips, events, and places through the lens of real spending.
3. Win by eliminating personal finance admin work and replacing fragmented tools with a trusted Safe-to-Spend system, a narrative-driven event model, place-aware context, and calm weekly guidance.

## What This Means in Practice

- short-term clarity first
- trust before deeper insight or sharing
- first useful answer fast, ideally in the first session
- frictionless capture and low-friction correction from day one
- context and guidance over raw data
- event, place, and narrative intelligence should reduce work, not add work
- social should be artifact-first, opt-in, and privacy-bounded
- privacy-first from day one
- core resilience from day one: safe degradation, manual fallback, and explicit freshness or confidence states
- less user labor, less guilt, less cleanup
- build a daily or weekly habit loop around overview, event, and story check-ins
- fast shipping with strong listening loops can still beat larger incumbents if the wedge is sharper and more memorable
