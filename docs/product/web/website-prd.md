# Website PRD

## Goal

The web lane exists to build trust, explain the Gama thesis, capture early demand, support future share surfaces at `gama.money`, and later support SEO or educational content. It is not the primary product surface.

## Web Jobs

- explain why Gama is different
- convert interested visitors into waitlist or early-access signups
- create privacy and trust confidence around financial and location data
- host future public or semi-public share artifacts such as event receipts or summaries
- support later SEO content without distorting the mobile roadmap

## Core Web Surfaces

- landing page
- waitlist or early-access page and thank-you flow
- privacy and disclosure pages
- future share-view pages for event receipts or summaries
- later educational or comparison content

## Current Foundation Status

The repo should maintain a production-minded website foundation before the actual marketing site is fully built:

- current official Next.js App Router plus Tailwind baseline
- premium landing, waitlist, share-view, and privacy surface foundation
- route-level metadata, sitemap, and robots support
- typed content structures that keep SEO and growth work grounded in product truth

The live waitlist backend, share hosting backends, and live analytics integrations remain intentionally deferred until MVP timing, disclosure, and operational ownership are ready.

## Messaging Constraints

The website must stay faithful to the actual product thesis: admin work elimination, forward-looking cash flow, reimbursements and shared spending correctness, Safe-to-Spend, privacy-first resilience, event and place intelligence, digital receipts, and calm weekly guidance.

The website must not imply:

- that Gama is already a public social network
- that users should publicly expose their finances
- that map or receipt sharing is automatic or public by default
